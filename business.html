Indian weddings:Financial Nightmare or Business Heaven

As the Covid restrictions die down, the Indian wedding season is returning with a vengeance. Based on a survey involving 35 cities and 4,302 service providers, the Confederation of All Indian Traders (CAIT) secretary general Praveen Khandelwal has projected 35 lakh weddings worth ₹3.75 lakh crore. These projections are for the period from November 4th to December 14th. This is an increase from last year’s 25 lakh weddings worth ₹3 lakh crore. This means that the number of weddings is expected to go up by 28% and the money spent by 25%. So, the “big fat Indian wedding” will only get bigger, but not really fat, as the pandemic sent it on a diet.
